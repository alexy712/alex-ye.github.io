## Aerodynamics & Structures – SAE Aero Airfoil Batch Analysis


### Project Duration – 
  August 2024 - Present


### Project Overview – 
  For Purdue SAE Aero, I was on the aerodynamics subteam. We were assigned with researching the most optimal airfoil for our competition requirements, along with the development and construction of the final wing design for the 2025 East Regular Class Division Competition in Fort Worth, Texas.


### Objectives –
  - Utilize XFLR5 to conduct parametric airfoil analyses to compare lift, drag, and stability characteristics across operating conditions for each airfoil in consideration.
  - Determine the airfoil that yields the best results for the club’s intended wing design for the aircraft being constructed. 


### Personal Role – 
  I developed a batch-processing workflow for airfoil analysis in XFLR5, enabling efficient comparison across candidate designs. The batch allowed for my team lead and other members to determine which airfoils would be best suited for the wing design we had come to a consensus on.

### Results –
  - Concluded that the Wortmann FX 63-137 airfoil was most optimal for our competition plane.
  - Developed an applied understanding of airfoil performance trade-offs under competition-specific Reynolds number constraints.
